**Question 1** [Easy] - Knowledge
Which of the following best describes the general mechanism of action of antiviral medications?

A) Directly killing viral organisms through cell wall lysis
B) Inhibiting viral replication at various stages of the viral life cycle
C) Stimulating the immune system to produce antibodies against viral antigens
D) Preventing viral attachment by coating host cell membranes

**Correct Answer: B**
**Rationale:** Antivirals work by inhibiting viral replication at various stages, such as viral entry, nucleic acid synthesis, or viral assembly and release. Viruses are not "killed" like bacteria because they are obligate intracellular parasites. While some antivirals may enhance immune response secondarily, their primary mechanism targets the replication cycle directly.

**Question 13** [Hard] - Knowledge

B) DAAs such as sofosbuvir/velpatasvir achieve sustained virologic response rates exceeding 95% in most genotypes with 8-12 weeks of therapy
C) DAAs are contraindicated in patients with compensated cirrhosis
D) DAAs primarily work by stimulating host immune response against hepatitis C

**Question 26** [Easy] - Knowledge
Which class of systemic antifungal agents includes ketoconazole, fluconazole, itraconazole, and voriconazole?

A) Echinocandins
B) Polyenes
C) Azoles
D) Allylamines

**Correct Answer: C**
**Rationale:** The azole antifungals are a major class that includes imidazoles (ketoconazole) and triazoles (fluconazole, itraconazole, voriconazole). They work by inhibiting the fungal enzyme lanosterol 14-alpha-demethylase (CYP51), which is essential for ergosterol synthesis in the fungal cell membrane. This disrupts membrane integrity and inhibits fungal growth. Azoles are widely used for both systemic and superficial fungal infections.

All systemic azole antifungals share which of the following safety concerns that necessitates baseline and periodic laboratory monitoring?

B) Hepatotoxicity requiring liver function test (LFT) monitoring

Which of the following best describes the mechanism by which azole antifungals cause significant drug interactions?

**Question 30** [Hard] - Case Study
A 58-year-old male on warfarin (INR target 2-3) for mechanical heart valve, simvastatin, and cyclosporine for a kidney transplant develops an esophageal candidiasis. The infectious disease consult recommends fluconazole. Which of the following is the NP's priority action?

B) Prescribe fluconazole but increase the warfarin dose prophylactically to prevent subtherapeutic INR

**Question 66** [Easy] - Knowledge
A KOH (potassium hydroxide) preparation is used in the diagnosis of fungal skin infections. What does a positive KOH prep demonstrate?

A) Bacterial colonies on agar plates
B) Fungal hyphae or yeast cells under microscopy after the KOH dissolves surrounding keratinocytes and debris
C) Viral inclusion bodies in skin cells
D) Elevated white blood cell count indicating infection

**Correct Answer: B**
**Rationale:** A KOH preparation involves applying 10-20% potassium hydroxide to a skin scraping on a glass slide.
