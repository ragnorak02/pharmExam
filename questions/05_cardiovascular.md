**Question 1** [Easy] - Knowledge
Which class of antihypertensive medications works by inhibiting the conversion of angiotensin I to angiotensin II?

A) Calcium channel blockers
B) ACE inhibitors
C) Beta blockers
D) Thiazide diuretics

**Correct Answer: B**
**Rationale:** ACE (angiotensin-converting enzyme) inhibitors block the enzyme that converts angiotensin I to angiotensin II. This reduces vasoconstriction and aldosterone secretion, thereby lowering blood pressure.

**Question 2** [Moderate] - Case Study
A 55-year-old female with newly diagnosed hypertension and type 2 diabetes presents for medication initiation. Her serum creatinine is 1.1 mg/dL and potassium is 4.2 mEq/L. Which antihypertensive is the most appropriate first-line choice?

A) Amlodipine 5 mg daily
B) Lisinopril 10 mg daily
C) Metoprolol 25 mg twice daily
D) Hydrochlorothiazide 25 mg daily

**Correct Answer: B**
**Rationale:** ACE inhibitors are preferred first-line agents for hypertensive patients with diabetes due to their renoprotective effects. They reduce intraglomerular pressure and slow the progression of diabetic nephropathy.

**Question 3** [Easy] - Knowledge
What is the most common reason patients discontinue ACE inhibitor therapy?

A) Hyperkalemia
B) Angioedema
C) Dry cough
D) First-dose hypotension

**Correct Answer: C**
**Rationale:** A persistent dry cough caused by the accumulation of bradykinin is the most common reason for discontinuation of ACE inhibitors. This occurs because ACE also degrades bradykinin; when ACE is inhibited, bradykinin accumulates in the lungs.

**Question 4** [Hard] - Case Study
A 48-year-old male on lisinopril 20 mg daily presents with a potassium level of 5.8 mEq/L. He reports recently starting a salt substitute to reduce sodium intake and has been taking an over-the-counter potassium supplement. Which combination of factors most likely contributed to his hyperkalemia?

A) ACE inhibitor + salt substitute + potassium supplement
B) ACE inhibitor + high-sodium diet + dehydration
C) ACE inhibitor + calcium supplement + vitamin D
D) ACE inhibitor + magnesium supplement + iron supplement

**Correct Answer: A**
**Rationale:** ACE inhibitors suppress aldosterone release, reducing potassium excretion. Salt substitutes contain potassium chloride, and the additional potassium supplement further increases the risk. Patients on ACE inhibitors should avoid potassium supplements and salt substitutes.

When should potassium and renal function be checked after initiating an ACE inhibitor?

B) Approximately 1 week after starting
C) 1 month after starting

**Correct Answer: B**
**Rationale:** Potassium and renal function should be checked approximately 1 week after starting an ACE inhibitor to monitor for hyperkalemia and worsening renal function, particularly in patients at risk.

**Question 6** [Hard] - Case Study
A 62-year-old female with bilateral renal artery stenosis is started on enalapril for hypertension. Two days later, she presents with oliguria and her serum creatinine has risen from 1.2 to 3.4 mg/dL. What is the most likely explanation?

A) Allergic interstitial nephritis from enalapril
B) Prerenal azotemia from dehydration
C) Loss of angiotensin II-mediated efferent arteriolar tone
D) Direct nephrotoxicity of enalapril

**Correct Answer: C**
**Rationale:** In bilateral renal artery stenosis, renal perfusion depends on angiotensin II-mediated constriction of the efferent arteriole. ACE inhibitors remove this compensatory mechanism, causing a precipitous drop in glomerular filtration pressure and acute kidney injury.

ACE inhibitors are contraindicated in which patient population due to the risk of major fetal malformations?

A) Elderly patients over 75 years
B) Pregnant women
C) Patients with gout

**Correct Answer: B**
**Rationale:** ACE inhibitors can cause major fetal malformations including renal defects, skull hypoplasia, and pulmonary hypoplasia. They are contraindicated in all trimesters of pregnancy.

**Question 9** [Moderate] - Knowledge
A patient on an ACE inhibitor is being considered for an additional antihypertensive. Which medication combination requires the MOST careful monitoring?

A) ACE inhibitor + thiazide diuretic
B) ACE inhibitor + potassium-sparing diuretic
C) ACE inhibitor + calcium channel blocker
D) ACE inhibitor + alpha blocker

**Correct Answer: B**
**Rationale:** Combining an ACE inhibitor with a potassium-sparing diuretic significantly increases the risk of hyperkalemia. ACE inhibitors suppress aldosterone, and potassium-sparing diuretics further reduce potassium excretion. This combination requires very careful potassium monitoring.

**Question 10** [Easy] - Knowledge
Which ACE inhibitor adverse effect is related to the inhibition of kinase II (bradykinin degradation)?

A) Hyperkalemia
B) Hypotension
C) Cough
D) Renal impairment

**Correct Answer: C**
**Rationale:** ACE is also known as kinase II when it degrades bradykinin. ACE inhibitors prevent bradykinin degradation, leading to its accumulation in the lungs, which stimulates the cough reflex.

**Question 11** [Moderate] - Case Study
A 58-year-old male with heart failure and an ejection fraction of 30% is currently on lisinopril, carvedilol, and furosemide. His blood pressure is 100/60 mmHg. The NP considers adding spironolactone. What is the primary concern with this addition?

A) Excessive hypotension
B) Hyperkalemia
C) Hyponatremia
D) Metabolic alkalosis

**Correct Answer: B**
**Rationale:** Spironolactone is an aldosterone antagonist that causes potassium retention. Combined with an ACE inhibitor (which also causes potassium retention by suppressing aldosterone), the risk of life-threatening hyperkalemia is significant. Potassium levels must be closely monitored.

**Question 12** [Hard] - Case Study

A) Switch to losartan
B) Switch to labetalol
C) Continue lisinopril and monitor closely
D) Switch to hydrochlorothiazide

**Correct Answer: B**
**Rationale:** Both ACE inhibitors and ARBs (losartan) are contraindicated in pregnancy. Labetalol is a beta blocker considered safe in pregnancy for hypertension management. Methyldopa is another option. The switch should occur before conception if possible.

What electrolyte should be monitored most carefully in a patient starting an ACE inhibitor?

B) Calcium

**Question 15** [Hard] - Case Study

**Question 18** [Moderate] - Case Study
A patient started on enalapril 5 mg experiences dizziness and a blood pressure drop to 85/55 mmHg after the first dose. The patient was previously on a high dose of furosemide. What is this phenomenon called?

A) Orthostatic hypotension
B) First-dose hypotension
C) Reflex tachycardia
D) Baroreceptor failure

**Correct Answer: B**
**Rationale:** ACE inhibitors can cause serious first-dose hypotension by causing a sharp drop in circulating angiotensin II. Patients who are volume-depleted (e.g., from diuretics) are at higher risk. Starting with a low dose and correcting volume depletion first can minimize this risk.

**Question 19** [Hard] - Knowledge
A patient is on lisinopril and develops a persistent cough. The NP decides to switch to an ARB. What is the primary pharmacologic reason ARBs do not typically cause cough?

A) ARBs do not affect the RAAS system
B) ARBs block angiotensin II at the receptor level without inhibiting bradykinin degradation
C) ARBs increase bradykinin levels less than ACE inhibitors
D) ARBs are metabolized differently and have no pulmonary effects

**Correct Answer: B**
**Rationale:** Unlike ACE inhibitors, ARBs block the action of angiotensin II at the AT1 receptor rather than inhibiting ACE. Since ACE (kinase II) also degrades bradykinin, ACE inhibitors cause bradykinin accumulation leading to cough, while ARBs do not affect bradykinin levels.

Calcium channel blockers are divided into two main categories. Which classification acts primarily on vascular smooth muscle with minimal cardiac effects?

B) Dihydropyridines

**Question 27** [Moderate] - Case Study

A) Nifedipine
B) Amlodipine
C) Verapamil
D) Felodipine

**Correct Answer: C**
**Rationale:** Verapamil (and diltiazem) suppress AV conduction and are useful for treating cardiac dysrhythmias in addition to hypertension and angina. Dihydropyridines (nifedipine, amlodipine, felodipine) do not have significant antidysrhythmic effects.

**Question 38** [Moderate] - Knowledge
In the heart, calcium channels are coupled to which receptors, making calcium channel blockade and beta blockade have identical cardiac effects?

B) Beta-1 receptors
C) Muscarinic receptors
D) Dopamine receptors

**Question 51** [Easy] - Knowledge
What is the mechanism of action of digoxin?

B) Inhibits Na+/K+-ATPase, increasing intracellular calcium

**Rationale:** Thrombolytic drugs (alteplase/tPA, streptokinase) convert plasminogen to plasmin, an enzyme that degrades the fibrin matrix of existing blood clots. They dissolve thrombi rather than preventing their formation.

**Question 156** [Moderate] - Knowledge

C) Both arterial and venous thrombi form identically
D) Arterial thrombi only form in atherosclerotic vessels

**Correct Answer: B**
